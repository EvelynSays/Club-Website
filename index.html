About Us

Step into the world of literature with Literary Larks, where every page turn promises a new adventure. As avid readers and passionate book enthusiasts, we gather monthly to explore the realms of imagination and storytelling. From timeless classics to contemporary gems, our book club invites you to embark on literary journeys that ignite the mind and stir the soul. Come visit new worlds with us every month, as we delve into the depths of prose, uncover hidden treasures within the pages, and share the joy of discovery with fellow members. Join us in celebrating the power of words and the boundless possibilities they offer.

Meeting Schedule

At Literary Larks, we convene once a month for our meetings, where each member takes turns submitting three book recommendations for consideration. Through a lively voting process, we select the winning book, embarking on a collective literary journey that enriches our minds and sparks insightful discussions.

We meet at Coffee @ 12 at 7pm on the last Friday of every Month.

Our Recommendations

Title The Library at Mount Char
Author Scott Hawkins
Description Amidst a mysterious disappearance and the quest for godlike power, Carolyn finds herself entangled in a battle for control over a library holding the secrets to the universe. Raised by a man known as Father, she and her siblings have been steeped in ancient customs and forbidden knowledge. But as Carolyn prepares for the looming confrontation, she realizes she's neglected her own humanity. With formidable adversaries and unexpected twists, "The Library at Mount Char" is a spellbinding blend of horror and humor, alien yet profoundly humanâ€”a gripping fantasy debut by a rising star.
Published Year 2015
Title Our Wives Under the Sea
Author Julia Armfield
Description When Leah returns from a disastrous deep-sea expedition, Miri believes she has her wife back. But Leah is different, haunted by whatever transpired in the depths. As they navigate a semblance of normalcy, Miri realizes their old life may be lost. Despite Leah's physical presence, Miri senses her slipping away. In "Our Wives Under The Sea," Julia Armfield crafts a poignant tale of love, grief, and the mysteries of the deep, following her acclaimed debut "Salt Slow."
Published Year 2022
Title A Psalm for the Wild-Built
Author Becky Chambers
Description Long ago, robots in Panga became self-aware, abandoned their tasks, and vanished into legend. Now, a tea monk's quiet life is disrupted by the unexpected arrival of a robot, bound by an ancient oath to inquire: "What do people truly need?" Yet, the answer proves elusive, varying with each perspective. As they search for clarity, Chambers' series probes the essence of desire in a world of abundance.
Published Year 2021
Title 1984
Author George Orwell
Description In George Orwell's timeless dystopian masterpiece, "1984," war is peace, freedom is slavery, and Big Brother is watching. Through the journey of one man's forbidden love affair in a world of totalitarian control, Orwell unveils a prophetic and haunting tale. As relevant as ever, "1984" delves into the destruction of truth, freedom, and individuality. This beautiful paperback edition, featuring deckled edges and French flaps, is a must-have for any bookshelf.
Published Year 1949
Title The Girl with All the Gifts
Author M.R. Carey
Description In "The Girl with All the Gifts," Melanie is no ordinary girl. Dubbed "our little genius" by Dr. Caldwell, she eagerly awaits each morning to be taken to class. Despite the cautious treatment by Sergeant Parks and his team, Melanie's love for learning knows no bounds. Yet, as she dreams of the future with her favorite teacher, Miss Justineau, she senses a sadness she can't quite understand. A sensational thriller reminiscent of Stephen King and Neil Gaiman, this novel captivates with its unique premise and gripping narrative.
Published Year 2014
